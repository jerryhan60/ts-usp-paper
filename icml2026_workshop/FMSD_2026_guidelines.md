# FMSD @ ICML 2026 Submission Guidelines

Source: https://icml-structured-fm-workshop.github.io/call-for-papers/
Downloaded: 2026-04-29

## Key Requirements

- Submission deadline: May 08, 2026, 11:59 pm AoE.
- Format: single PDF on OpenReview using the ICML 2026 LaTeX style template.
- Page limit: up to 4 pages for the main paper; references and appendices are unlimited.
- Review: double-blind. Submissions that exceed the page limit or are not properly anonymized may be desk-rejected.
- Impact statement: not required for the workshop submission.
- Workshop is non-archival. Accepted papers appear on OpenReview and the workshop website, but there are no formally published proceedings.
- ICML 2026 main-conference accepted papers are not allowed.
- Papers under review elsewhere may be submitted.
- Previously published papers should be reasonably extended.

## Scope Notes

- In scope: foundation-model approaches for structured data, especially tabular and time-series objectives such as forecasting, classification, regression, and structured generation.
- LLM/agent work is in scope only when evaluation centers on predictive tabular/time-series objectives and includes strong classical baselines, cost/latency reporting, and numerical reliability checks.
- Out of scope: general-purpose foundation-model architectures without a clear structured-data focus, and NLP-centric table tasks such as table QA or semantic parsing.

## Local Compliance Checklist

- Uses `icml2026` style: yes.
- Anonymous author block: yes.
- Main-paper target: references should begin after the first 4 pages.
- Impact statement: remove unless needed, since it is not required and counts against the main-page budget.
